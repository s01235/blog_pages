Iterative Learning of Human Partner’s Desired Trajectory for Proactive Human-Robot Collaboration

IDEA

1、Issue to be addressed

Although a repetitive human-robot collaboration task is considered, the task period is subject to uncertainty introduced by the human.

2、Main Work

A novel learning mechanism is proposed to achieve the control objective. Theoretical analysis. Selective simulations and experiments.

3、Contributions

  1. Based on a simplified human arm model, estimation of the human partner’s desired trajectory is formulated as a force regulation problem.
  2. ILC is employed to achieve force regulation, thus obtaining the human partner;s desired trajectory through repetitive pHRI.
  3. A period-varying ILC is developed to deal with the problem of interactions with uncertain time durations due to human variance, so it can be applied to a wider class of pHRI applications.
